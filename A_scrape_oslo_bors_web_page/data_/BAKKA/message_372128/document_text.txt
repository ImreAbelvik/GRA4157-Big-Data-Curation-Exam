The Board of Directors of Bakkafrost has decided to 
continue with the share saving plan for employees in 
2015. The share saving plan for employees was 
started in 2014.

The following primary insiders have subscribed to 
the share saving plan and accordingly purchased 
Bakkafrost shares:

Regin Jacobsen, CEO, purchased 198 shares. After the 
transaction he holds 4,492,409 shares.

Gunnar Nielsen, CFO, purchased 64 shares. After the 
transaction he holds 64 shares.

Odd Eliasen, Managing Director, purchased 165 
shares. After the transaction he holds 170,846 
shares.

Annika Frederiksberg, Board Member, purchased 71 
shares. After the transaction she holds directly and 
indirectly 14,603 shares.

The shares were purchased from P/F Bakkafrost. In 
addition to the shares sold to the primary insiders, 
P/F Bakkafrost sold 4,530 to other employees as part 
of the share saving plan. In total P/F Bakkafrost 
sold 5,028 shares and after the sale P/F Bakkafrost 
holds 332,300 treasury shares. All the shares have 
been acquired at a price of NOK 161 per share.

About Bakkafrost:
Bakkafrost is the largest salmon farmer in the Faroe 
Islands. The Group is fully integrated from feed 
production to smolt, farming, VAP and sales. The 
Group has production of fish meal, fish oil and 
salmon feed in Fuglafjørður. The Group operates 
licenses on 14 farming fjords. The Group has primary 
processing in Klaksvík and Kollafjørð and secondary 
processing (VAP) in Glyvrar and Fuglafjørður. The 
headquarters are located in Glyvrar, and the company 
has a total of 700 employees.
