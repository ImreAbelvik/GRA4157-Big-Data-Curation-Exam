Reference is made to the announcement from Induct AS (the "Company") on 18 July
2019 regarding result of Preferential Rights Issue.

NPP Capital AS, a company owned 100% by Alf Martin Johansen, CEO of Induct,
subscribed for and was allocated 211,997 shares at a subscription price of NOK 5
per share in the Preferential Rights Issue. Total holding after the transaction:
1,361,838 shares and subscription rights to subscribe to 327,401 shares at a
subscription price of NOK 5 per share. The subscription rights are exercisable
between 1 and 15 February 2020.

Key Partners AS, a company controlled by Åge Muren, CFO & General Counsel of
Induct, subscribed for and was allocated 20,000 shares at a subscription price
of NOK 5 per share in the Preferential Rights Issue. Total holding after the
transaction: 252,125 shares and subscription rights to subscribe to 60,000
shares at a subscription price of NOK 5 per share. The subscription rights are
exercisable between 1 and 15 February 2020.

In addition Åge Muren holds 35,000 share options in Induct, which are fully
vested, with an exercise price of NOK 20 per share, exercisable until 29
September 2020. Reference is made to the announcement by the Company on 30
September 2016 for further details.

About Induct

Based on ten years of collaboration with more than 250 organizations, Induct
offers collaborative communities, delivered as "Software as a Service," that
enable organizations to digitalize and manage business processes. This also
include create, manage, track and measure the innovation process from idea
creation through to final implementation and impact reporting. Induct enables
its customers to connect with each other in larger networks to share best
practices, while deploying and monetizing initiatives - all within a secure,
collaborative, and access-controlled cloud-based network.

Induct is listed on the Merkur Market list on the Oslo Stock Exchange with the
ticker INDUCT-ME.
